MD445 - STOURBRIDGE JUNCTION TO STOURBRIDGE TOWN
STOURBRIDGE JN To STOURBRIDGE TOWN

(OTHER THAN PRE-METRO OPERATIONS)
No traction unit with a brake defect is to be allowed to travel in the Down direction from Stourbridge Junction to
Stourbridge Town. Under no circumstances must single car diesel multiple units, other than Class 153 units operate
between Stourbridge Junction and Stourbridge Town stations.
Working of Class 153 Diesel Multiple Units. In the event of the failure of the track circuit actuator on a Class 153 Diesel
Multiple Unit working between Stourbridge Junction and Stourbridge Town, the unit may continue in service normally
between these points.
When the unit has completed its diagram between these points it must be worked E.C.S. to a maintenance depot in
accordance with the instructions contained in Rule Book, Module TW5, Section 21.
An Annetts Key Lock is provided to operate Ground Frame Lever No.1 controlling the entrance/exit from the Stourbridge
Town Branch. The key forms an integral part of the train staff that is provided for the Stourbridge Town Branch.
The Conductor is authorised to transfer the train staff between the Train Staff Release Cabinet and the Driver in
accordance with Rule Book, Module TS8, Regulation 2.
The ground frame will be operated by the Conductor. The Driver of a train waiting to enter/leave the Stourbridge Town
Branch must await advice from the Conductor that the ground frame is correctly set for the safe movement of the train.

MD450 - STOURBRIDGE NORTH JUNCTION TO ROUND OAK
Kingswinford Jn South To Round Oak Sidings
Working Of Down Round Oak 1 and Up Round Oak 2 Sidings
Arriving Trains
Arriving trains will normally be signalled from signal DR5703 at Kingswinford Junction over the Down Round Oak Siding 1
towards the “Stop And Await Instructions” board located on the Down Round Oak Siding 1 at Round Oak.
The DB Schenker Person In Charge (PIC) will authorise movements past the “Stop And Await Instructions” board located
on the Down Round Oak Siding 1. Once the train has past the “Stop and Await Instructions” board, the DB Schenker PIC
must contact the Signaller at the WMSC Stourbridge Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) and confirm the train is clear of the
“Stop And Await Instructions” board located on the Down Round Oak Siding 1, complete with tail lamp.
Departing Trains
Prior to any train movement departing towards the Up Round Oak Siding 2 or Down Round Oak Siding 1, the DB
Schenker PIC must contact the Signaller at WMSC Stourbridge Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) and advise which siding
the train is to depart on and obtain permission for the train to proceed to either “Stop And Contact Signaller” board
DR7718 on the Up Round Oak Siding 2 or “Stop And Contact Signaller” board DR9720 on the Down Round Oak Siding 1.
On arrival at either “Stop And Contact Signaller” board DR7718 on the Up Round Oak Siding 2 or “Stop And Contact
Signaller” board DR9720 on the Down Round Oak Siding 1, the Driver must contact the Signaller at WMSC Stourbridge
Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) and obtain permission to proceed.
When the train has departed, the DB Schenker PIC must secure No 3 handpoints towards the Round Oak Siding.
Running Round a Train Via Kingswinford Junction
A locomotive required to run round a train at Round Oak will be routed over Down Round Oak Siding 1. The DB Schenker
PIC will contact the Signaller at WMSC Stourbridge Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) and obtain permission for the
locomotive to proceed towards the “Stop And Contact Signaller” board DR9720 on the Down Round Oak Siding 1.
On arrival at “Stop And Contact Signaller” board DR9720 on the Down Round Oak Siding 1, the Driver must contact the
Signaller at WMSC Stourbridge Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) and obtain permission to proceed.
At Kingswinford Junction the locomotive shall be routed towards the Up Dudley line behind signal DR1705 and will be
signalled over the Up Round Oak Siding 2 towards the “Stop Proceed Only When The Route Is Clear Throughout” board
at Round Oak.
The DB Schenker PIC is in charge of all movements beyond the “Stop Proceed Only When The Route Is Clear
Throughout” board at Round Oak.
The DB Schenker PIC must contact the Signaller at WMSC Stourbridge Workstation (Tel: 0121 576 5711) when the
locomotive has past clear of the “Stop Proceed Only When The Route Is Clear Throughout” board and is complete with tail
lamp.
